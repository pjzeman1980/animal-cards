African Elephant

Elephants are the only mammals that cannot jump due to their large size.

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana
  • Average Length: 20 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 70 years
  • Habitat: African savannahs

African elephants are the largest terrestrial animal, standing up to 13 feet tall and weighing 11.5 tons. They have long tusks up to eleven feet long and a trunk which is used to lift and manipulate heavy objects. African elephants live up to 70 years in the wild, and live in herds of 6 to 70 members. They are herbivores subsisting on leaves, herbs, and other plants.
